NELSON ROLIHLAHLA MANDELA

The father of Modern South Africa
Successfully leading the resistance to South Africa's policy of apartheid in the 20th centur

Here's a time line of Nelson Mandela

  • 1918 Bron in village of Mvezo in Transkei
  • 1943 Graduates with BA from Fort Hare; Enrols for an LLB at Wits University
  • 1944 Forms the A.N.C. Youth League
  • 1952 Starts Law Practice
  • 1956 Arrested on Treason Charges
  • 1960 Killings in Sharpeville
  • 1962 Mandela Arrested Again
  • 1963 Government Raid in Rivonia
  • 1964 Sentenced to Life in Prison
  • 1976 Thousands of Students Protest Afrikaans Language in Soweto
  • 1982 Moved From an Isolated Prison
  • 1985 Secret Meetings With Government Begin
  • 1988 Transferred to Another Prison
  • 1990 Ban Lifted on A.N.C.; Mandela's Release Promised
  • 1993 Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize with President FW de Klerk
  • 1994 Votes for the first time in his life and Elected by Parliament as first president of a democratic South Africa
  • 1999 Steps down after one term as President, establishes the Nelson Mandela Foundation
  • 2004 Announces that he will be stepping down from public life
  • 2013 Passes away at home in Johannesburg at the age of 95

"Nelson Mandela endured great personal hardship so that others could be provided with dignity, equality and opportunity. He fought discrimination and inhuman exclusion, but rose above bitter divisions to heal and reconcile a fractured nation. His life and work made him a citizen of the world"

Former Prime Minister Of India Dr. Manmohan Singh.
